M

Hi, I am Muddenahalli.

I am the red earth and the quiet hills of Sathya Sai Grama. I was here when this was only a silent wilderness, and I am here now as it serves the world. I am not just a place; I am the mother of this mission. I have felt every teardrop of sacrifice and every heartbeat of love. My story is written in the lives of those who gave everything to me.

01

The Missionary

I remember when my dust was first stirred by two brothers who walked as one. I felt the fire of Narayan Bhat and the unwavering strength of Narasimha Murthy and fellow thiagajeevis. They had no money, only a shared dream. I felt their sweat fall upon me as they built the first walls, sharing a single vision to feed and teach the children. They didn't just walk on me; they offered their lives to my soil so that a future could take root.

02

The Master & The Missionary

Then came the 'Sun'—Bhagawan Sri Sathya Sai Baba. I felt my hills glow with a divine light when He walked upon me. I saw the moment Anna surrendered this entire land at His Lotus Feet. The Master promised, "You look after My work, and I will look after you." I was no longer just a hilltop; I became a land of Love. I watched the Master take that small seed and turn it into a Divine Mandate.

03

The Missionary, The Master, The Man & The Mission

I saw the perfect union manifest on this land: the body of Madhusudan Sai, the soul of Narayana Bhat, and the consciousness of Sathya Sai Baba. This divine synthesis sparked a great movement. It was no longer the journey of a single individual, but of an army of missionary men and women. Inspired by this trinity, they carried the torch forward, building institutions of healing and learning across the globe. The stream of service flowed through thousands of hands, all beating with one heart.

04

The Missionary, The Master, The Man, The Children & The Future

Now, I look toward the horizon and I see an explosion of light. I see my "plants"—the students—now standing tall as the new Narayan Bhats and Narasimha Murthys. The spirit of Anna is no longer just in my soil; it is in the hands of the doctor healing a heart and the teacher lighting a lamp in a faraway village. I see a future where no child is hungry and no soul is forgotten. The Missionary, the Master, and the Man have laid the path—now, they live through The Men and The Women of tomorrow. I am Muddenahalli, and I am watching the world become one family.
